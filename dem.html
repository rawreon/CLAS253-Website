Athenian democracy was unique in ancient Greece as the other forms of government at the time consisted of monarchies and oligarchies.

“Our constitution is called a democracy because power is in the hands not of a minority but of the whole people. ” –Pericles(Thucydides).

It gave power to the majority of people instead of a few individuals, and it was very direct. Massive assemblies made political decisions and presided over trials. There was very little representative democracy. Such a unique political structure is surely worth studying today, especially since we live in a democracy.

Civic engagement was important back then, and even more now. We may learn much by examining the successes and failures of Western civilization’s first democracy.
